University of Mannheim, Germany

Chair of Statistics
Research Associate (3/2017 - present)
Research Assistant (6/2016 - 2/2017)

Research Training Group (RTG) on Statistical Modeling of Complex Systems and Processes, University of Mannheim and University of Heidelberg
Research Student (2/2016 - 2/2017)

Supervisor: Professor Carsten Jentsch

My work in these two positions is mostly theoretical, in particular I have been involved in***:

  • Theoretical development of high dimensional time series models and their application to model dynamic networks.
  • Fitting high dimensional models with fast and stable algorithms.

Motivation to work in this area came after I took some courses on high dimensional statistics and machine learning. My current tasks include understanding high dimensional time series models. In particular, theoretical details, proof techniques and exploring further development. With the theoretical work we also need an efficient way or algorithm to fit the models with data sets. That explains the other part of my tasks, which is the fitting problem. I mostly use R. Occasionally I switch to Python for faster implementation. Current plan is to use Rcpp package in R.

***Please note, for all positions mentioned above, I have done similar tasks with same supervisor.


Chair of Econometrics
Research Assistant (3/2015 - 2/2017)

Supervisor: Professor Markus Frölich

Here my tasks were mostly related to data cleaning and data preparation for different development projects. In short my tasks were,

  • Cleaning and transforming raw data sets (what we received directly from paper/telephone surveys) to a usable format so that the principal investogators could do analysis as quickly as possible.
  • Running basic analysis or checks, e.g., fitting simple OLS, checking significance levels and producing plots/tables.
  • Proof-reading different works from the chair, in particular draft version of journal or working papers, on-line documents and browse for relevant literatures.

For small implementation I used STATA and for medium to large implementations sometimes I switched to Python. At the time I left the chair, almost allprojects that I have been involved were still in work in progress. You can see an IZA discussion paper, "Discussion Paper No. 9207" from one of the projects that I was involved with.
