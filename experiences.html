University of Mannheim, Germany

Chair of Statistics
Research Associate (3/2017 - present)
Research Assistant (6/2016 - 2/2017)

Research Training Group (RTG) on Statistical Modeling of Complex Systems and Processes, University of Mannheim and University of Heidelberg
Research Student (2/2016 - 2/2017)

Supervisor: Dr. Carsten Jentsch

In particular I have been involved in

  • Theoretical development of large time series models and their application to model dynamic networks.
  • Fitting models with fast and stable algorithms.

My tasks include understanding multivariate time series models (VARs) where we consider many varaibles together. In paractice, classical VAR models are not suitable for more than 4 or 5 variables (because of computational issues and also we lose degrees of freedom very quickly). But with recent developments in high dimensional statistics, specaially in regularization and penalization, we can analyze large var models. I am looking at theoretical details, proof techniques and exploring further developments for large VAR models where the coefficient matrix could be sparse. Besides theoretical understandings, I am also running simulations and implementing algorithms to fit the model with data sets. Mostly I use R for implementation, but occasionally I switch to Python for faster performance.
