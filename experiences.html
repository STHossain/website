Research Training Group (RTG) on Statistical Modeling of Complex Systems and Processes, University of Mannheim and University of Heidelberg

Research Student (2/2016 - 2/2017)

Supervisor: Professor Carsten Jentsch

In particular I was working with
  • Theoretical development of large time series models and their application to model dynamic networks.
  • Fitting models with fast and stable algorithms.

My tasks were mostly focused on understanding multivariate time series models (VARs) with many variables together. In practice, classical VAR models are not suitable for more than 4 or 5 variables (because of computational issues and also we face loss of degrees of freedom very quickly). But with recent developments in high dimensional statistics, specially in regularization and penalization, we can analyze large VAR models with these methods. In this perios I was looking at theoretical details, proof techniques and exploring further developments for large VAR models where the coefficient matrix could be sparse. Besides theoretical understandings, my tasks also included running simulations and implementing algorithms to fit different models with data sets. I used mostly R for implementation.
